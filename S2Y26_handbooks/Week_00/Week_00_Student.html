SCENARIO

You’ve been hired as a junior data analyst at a marine research institute. Your supervisor has asked you to verify some calculations from a recent fish population study before the data goes into a publication.

The study uses the Schaefer growth model:

\[G(S) = g \cdot S \cdot \left(1 - \frac{S}{K}\right)\]

where: - \(G(S)\) = annual growth (tonnes/year) - \(S\) = current stock (tonnes)
- \(g = 0.4\) (intrinsic growth rate) - \(K = 2000\) (carrying capacity in tonnes)

Your supervisor has provided three stock measurements from different years and their computed growth values. She wants you to verify the calculations and plot the growth function.

Data provided:

Year Stock \(S\) (tonnes) Reported \(G(S)\) (tonnes/year)
2018 400 128
2020 1000 200
2022 1600 128

YOUR TASK

Part A: Manual Verification (15 minutes)

  1. Using BODMAS and the formula above, calculate \(G(400)\), \(G(1000)\), and \(G(1600)\) by hand. Do your values match the reported data?

  2. Expand \(G(S) = g \cdot S \cdot (1 - S/K)\) algebraically to show it’s a quadratic in \(S\). Write it in standard form \(G(S) = aS^2 + bS + c\) and identify the coefficients \(a\), \(b\), \(c\).

  3. At what stock level is growth maximized? (Hint: Use the vertex formula for a quadratic.)

Part B: Python Implementation (25 minutes)

  1. Write a Python function schaefer_growth(S, g, K) that computes \(G(S)\).

  2. Create an array of stock values from \(S = 0\) to \(S = 2000\) using np.linspace().

  3. Plot the growth function \(G(S)\) against stock \(S\). Label your axes appropriately and add a title.

  4. Add vertical lines or markers to show where the three data points fall on your curve.

Part C: Interpretation (15 minutes)

  1. Your supervisor asks: “If we want the fishery to produce the maximum sustainable yield, what stock level should we target?” Write a 2–3 sentence answer based on your analysis.

  2. The fishing industry currently harvests 150 tonnes/year. At the 2022 stock level (\(S = 1600\)), is this harvest sustainable? Why or why not?

DELIVERABLES

HINTS / SCAFFOLDING

BODMAS reminder: - Brackets, Orders (powers), Division/Multiplication, Addition/Subtraction

Vertex of quadratic \(y = ax^2 + bx + c\): - Maximum/minimum at \(x = -\frac{b}{2a}\)

Python starter:

import numpy as np
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt

def schaefer_growth(S, g, K):
    return g * S * (1 - S/K)

S = np.linspace(0, 2000, 500)
G = schaefer_growth(S, 0.4, 2000)

plt.plot(S, G)
plt.xlabel('Stock (tonnes)')
plt.ylabel('Growth (tonnes/year)')
plt.title('Schaefer Growth Model')
plt.show()
