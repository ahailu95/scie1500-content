Week 8: Lotka-Volterra and Predator-Prey Dynamics

PROBLEM BRIEF: W8

“Feral Cat Management: Protecting Native Wildlife”

SCENARIO

The Western Shield program aims to protect endangered numbats from feral cats. Ecologists have developed a predator-prey model:

\[\begin{aligned} \frac{dN}{dt} &= 0.4N - 0.002NC \\ \frac{dC}{dt} &= -0.3C + 0.001NC \end{aligned}\]

where: - \(N\) = numbat population - \(C\) = feral cat population - \(t\) = time (years)

Model interpretation: - Numbats grow at 40% per year without cats - Each cat-numbat interaction reduces numbat growth - Cats decline at 30% per year without prey - Predation increases cat population

YOUR TASK

Part A: Equilibrium Analysis (25 minutes)

  1. Find the trivial equilibrium (both species extinct):
    • Set \(N = 0, C = 0\)
  2. Find the coexistence equilibrium:
    • Set \(\frac{dN}{dt} = 0\): \(N(0.4 - 0.002C) = 0\)
      • Either \(N = 0\) or \(C = ?\)
    • Set \(\frac{dC}{dt} = 0\): \(C(-0.3 + 0.001N) = 0\)
      • Either \(C = 0\) or \(N = ?\)
    • Coexistence: \((N^*, C^*) = (?, ?)\)
  3. Interpret: What does this equilibrium mean for wildlife management?

Part B: Phase Plane Analysis (20 minutes)

  1. Nullclines (curves where population growth = 0):

    • Numbat nullcline: \(\frac{dN}{dt} = 0\) → \(N = 0\) or \(C = ?\)
    • Cat nullcline: \(\frac{dC}{dt} = 0\) → \(C = 0\) or \(N = ?\)

  2. The phase plane is divided into four regions:

    Region Condition \(\frac{dN}{dt}\) \(\frac{dC}{dt}\) Movement
    I \(N < N^*, C < C^*\) ? ? ?
    II \(N > N^*, C < C^*\) ? ? ?
    III \(N > N^*, C > C^*\) ? ? ?
    IV \(N < N^*, C > C^*\) ? ? ?

    Fill in the table.

  3. Sketch the phase portrait showing how populations move in each region.

Part C: Management Interventions (15 minutes)

  1. Cat culling: Remove 50 cats per year
    • Modified equation: \(\frac{dC}{dt} = -0.3C + 0.001NC - 50\)
    • Find the new equilibrium
    • How does numbat population change?
  2. Numbat translocation: Add 20 numbats per year
    • Modified equation: \(\frac{dN}{dt} = 0.4N - 0.002NC + 20\)
    • Find the new equilibrium
    • Compare effectiveness to cat culling
  3. Which intervention better supports numbat conservation? Why?

Python template:

import numpy as np
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt

# Equilibrium
N_star = 300
C_star = 200

# Nullclines
N_range = np.linspace(0, 600, 100)
C_range = np.linspace(0, 400, 100)

plt.figure(figsize=(10, 8))
plt.axhline(y=C_star, color='blue', linewidth=2, linestyle='--', label=f'Numbat nullcline (C={C_star})')
plt.axvline(x=N_star, color='red', linewidth=2, linestyle='--', label=f'Cat nullcline (N={N_star})')
plt.plot(N_star, C_star, 'go', markersize=15, label=f'Equilibrium ({N_star}, {C_star})')

# Direction field
N_grid = np.linspace(50, 550, 15)
C_grid = np.linspace(50, 350, 12)
N_mesh, C_mesh = np.meshgrid(N_grid, C_grid)

dN_dt = 0.4 * N_mesh - 0.002 * N_mesh * C_mesh
dC_dt = -0.3 * C_mesh + 0.001 * N_mesh * C_mesh

# Normalize arrows
magnitude = np.sqrt(dN_dt**2 + dC_dt**2)
dN_norm = dN_dt / (magnitude + 1e-10)
dC_norm = dC_dt / (magnitude + 1e-10)

plt.quiver(N_mesh, C_mesh, dN_norm, dC_norm, alpha=0.5)

plt.xlabel('Numbat Population (N)')
plt.ylabel('Feral Cat Population (C)')
plt.title('Predator-Prey Phase Plane')
plt.legend()
plt.grid(True, alpha=0.3)
plt.xlim(0, 600)
plt.ylim(0, 400)
plt.show()

“Fox Control on the Mainland: Protecting Quokka Colonies”

SCENARIO

On the Australian mainland, quokka (Setonix brachyurus) populations face severe predation pressure from the European red fox (Vulpes vulpes). While quokkas thrive on Rottnest Island (which has no foxes), mainland colonies are critically endangered. Ecologists model this predator-prey system:

$ \[\begin{aligned} \frac{dQ}{dt} &= 0.3Q - 0.003QF \\ \frac{dF}{dt} &= -0.2F + 0.0005QF \end{aligned}\]

$

where:

  • $Q$ = quokka population (number of individuals)
  • $F$ = fox population (number of individuals)
  • $t$ = time in years

Model features:

  • Quokkas grow at 30% per year without predation (moderate reproductive rate)
  • Foxes decline at 20% per year without prey
  • Predation interactions affect both populations

YOUR TASK

Part A: Finding Equilibria (25 minutes)

  1. Trivial equilibrium:

    • $(Q, F) = (0, 0)$ — both species absent from the mainland
    • Is this realistic given the current state of mainland populations?

  2. Coexistence equilibrium:

    • Set $ = 0$ : $Q(0.3 - 0.003F) = 0$
      • Either $Q = 0$ or $F = 100$
    • Set $ = 0$ : $F(-0.2 + 0.0005Q) = 0$
      • Either $F = 0$ or $Q = 400$
    • Equilibrium: $(Q^*, F^*) = (400, 100)$

  3. Stability consideration:

    • Are populations close to equilibrium stable?
    • Or do they cycle (oscillate)?
    • In this model, small perturbations cause oscillations

Part B: Nullclines and Phase Portrait (20 minutes)

  1. Nullclines:

    • Quokka nullcline: $Q = 0$ or $F = 100$
    • Fox nullcline: $F = 0$ or $Q = 400$

  2. Create a phase plane table:

    Region $Q$ vs. 400 $F$ vs. 100 $$ $$ Trajectory
    I $Q < 400$ $F < 100$ + − Q increases, F decreases
    II $Q > 400$ $F < 100$ + + Both increase
    III $Q > 400$ $F > 100$ − + Q decreases, F increases
    IV $Q < 400$ $F > 100$ − − Both decrease

  3. Sketch the cyclic behavior:

    • Trajectories spiral around the equilibrium
    • High quokkas → foxes increase → quokkas decline → foxes decline → cycle repeats

Part C: Management Scenarios (15 minutes)

  1. Fox baiting program: 1080 poison baiting removes 10 foxes/year

    • New equation: $ = -0.2F + 0.0005QF - 10$
    • Find new equilibrium: $-0.2F + 0.0005QF - 10 = 0$
    • From prey nullcline: $F = 100$ still. Substitute: $-0.2(100) + 0.0005Q(100) - 10 = 0$
    • Solve: $-20 + 0.05Q - 10 = 0 Q = 600$
    • New equilibrium: $(Q^*, F^*) = (600, 100)$

  2. Quokka translocation: Reintroduce 15 quokkas/year from Rottnest Island

    • New equation: $ = 0.3Q - 0.003QF + 15$
    • From predator nullcline: $Q = 400$ still. Substitute: $0.3(400) - 0.003(400)F + 15 = 0$
    • Solve: $120 - 1.2F + 15 = 0 F = 112.5$
    • New equilibrium: $(Q^*, F^*) = (400, 112.5)$

  3. Compare the two strategies:

    • Fox baiting: Quokkas increase 50% (400 → 600), foxes unchanged at 100
    • Quokka translocation: Quokkas stay at 400, foxes increase to 112.5 — the extra quokkas just feed more foxes!
    • Write a brief (3-4 sentence) recommendation explaining why predator control is more effective than prey supplementation for this system

PYTHON TEMPLATE

import numpy as np
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt
from scipy.integrate import odeint

# Define the system
def predator_prey(state, t):
    Q, F = state
    dQ_dt = 0.3 * Q - 0.003 * Q * F
    dF_dt = -0.2 * F + 0.0005 * Q * F
    return [dQ_dt, dF_dt]

# Equilibrium
Q_star, F_star = 400, 100

# Phase plane
Q_range = np.linspace(50, 800, 15)
F_range = np.linspace(10, 250, 15)
Q_mesh, F_mesh = np.meshgrid(Q_range, F_range)

dQ_dt = 0.3 * Q_mesh - 0.003 * Q_mesh * F_mesh
dF_dt = -0.2 * F_mesh + 0.0005 * Q_mesh * F_mesh

# Normalize
magnitude = np.sqrt(dQ_dt**2 + dF_dt**2)
dQ_norm = dQ_dt / (magnitude + 1e-10)
dF_norm = dF_dt / (magnitude + 1e-10)

plt.figure(figsize=(10, 8))

# Nullclines
plt.axhline(y=100, color='blue', linewidth=2, linestyle='--', label='Quokka nullcline (F=100)')
plt.axvline(x=400, color='red', linewidth=2, linestyle='--', label='Fox nullcline (Q=400)')
plt.plot(Q_star, F_star, 'go', markersize=15, label=f'Equilibrium ({Q_star}, {F_star})')

# Vector field
plt.quiver(Q_mesh, F_mesh, dQ_norm, dF_norm, alpha=0.5)

# Add a trajectory
t = np.linspace(0, 50, 1000)
initial_state = [500, 60]
trajectory = odeint(predator_prey, initial_state, t)
plt.plot(trajectory[:, 0], trajectory[:, 1], 'purple', linewidth=1.5, label='Sample trajectory')

plt.xlabel('Quokka Population (Q)')
plt.ylabel('Fox Population (F)')
plt.title('Predator-Prey Phase Plane: Quokkas vs. Foxes')
plt.legend()
plt.grid(True, alpha=0.3)
plt.xlim(0, 900)
plt.ylim(0, 300)
plt.show()

“Dingo Conservation: Managing Kangaroo Populations”

SCENARIO

In arid Australia, dingoes (Canis dingo) are apex predators that regulate kangaroo populations. Removal of dingoes often leads to kangaroo overabundance and rangeland degradation. Ecologists model this system:

$ \[\begin{aligned} \frac{dK}{dt} &= 0.5K - 0.0015KD \\ \frac{dD}{dt} &= -0.25D + 0.0008KD \end{aligned}\]

$

where:

  • $K$ = kangaroo population (number of individuals)
  • $D$ = dingo population (number of individuals)
  • $t$ = time in years

Model features:

  • Kangaroos grow at 50% per year without predation (high reproductive rate)
  • Dingoes decline at 25% per year without prey
  • Predation interactions affect both populations

YOUR TASK

Part A: Finding Equilibria (25 minutes)

  1. Trivial equilibrium:

    • $(K, D) = (0, 0)$ — both species extinct
    • Is this realistic for arid Australia?

  2. Coexistence equilibrium:

    • Set $ = 0$ : $K(0.5 - 0.0015D) = 0$
      • Either $K = 0$ or $D = 333.33$ (round to 333)
    • Set $ = 0$ : $D(-0.25 + 0.0008K) = 0$
      • Either $D = 0$ or $K = 312.5$ (round to 313)
    • Equilibrium: $(K^*, D^*) = (313, 333)$

  3. Stability consideration:

    • Are populations close to equilibrium stable?
    • Or do they cycle (oscillate)?
    • In this model, small perturbations cause oscillations

Part B: Nullclines and Phase Portrait (20 minutes)

  1. Nullclines:

    • Kangaroo nullcline: $K = 0$ or $D = 333$
    • Dingo nullcline: $D = 0$ or $K = 313$

  2. Create a phase plane table:

    Region $K$ vs. 313 $D$ vs. 333 $$ $$ Trajectory
    I $K < 313$ $D < 333$ + − K increases, D decreases
    II $K > 313$ $D < 333$ + + Both increase
    III $K > 313$ $D > 333$ − + K decreases, D increases
    IV $K < 313$ $D > 333$ − − Both decrease

  3. Sketch the cyclic behavior:

    • Trajectories spiral around the equilibrium
    • High kangaroos → dingoes increase → kangaroos decline → dingoes decline → cycle repeats

Part C: Management Scenarios (15 minutes)

  1. Dingo culling: Pastoralists remove 40 dingoes/year to protect livestock

    • New equation: $ = -0.25D + 0.0008KD - 40$
    • Find new equilibrium: $-0.25D + 0.0008KD - 40 = 0$
    • If $D = 333$ : $-0.25(333) + 0.0008K(333) - 40 = 0$
    • Solve for new $K$ equilibrium

  2. Effect of culling:

    • How much does kangaroo equilibrium increase?
    • What are the consequences for rangeland health?

  3. Alternative: Dingo protection

    • No culling: equilibrium remains at $(313, 333)$
    • Lower kangaroo numbers reduce grazing pressure
    • Write a brief (3-4 sentence) recommendation on dingo management for sustainable rangeland use

# Phase plane
K_range = np.linspace(50, 600, 15)
